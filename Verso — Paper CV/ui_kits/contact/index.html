About

A printer, slightly out of her century.

I learned to bind books before I learned to drive, and I have never quite caught up with the modern world — happily.

My studio sits in a converted dairy in the Five Valleys, full of cast-iron presses and drawers of metal type rescued from closing-down sales. I set everything by hand: it is slower, it is fussier, and it is the only way I know to make a page feel inevitable.

I take on a small number of private commissions each year — wedding readings, memorial editions, the odd stubbornly beautiful poem. If you have something worth pressing into paper, write to me.

— Eleanor
